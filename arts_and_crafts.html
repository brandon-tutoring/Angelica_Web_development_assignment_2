1. Easy Resin Glitter Necklace

What you will need.

  • Gedeo Crystal Resin package or:
    • Resin
    • Hardener
    • Measuring Cups
    • Stir Sticks
    • Plastic Gloves
  • Glitter!!
  • Resin Jewelry Molds
  • Mini Long-Nose Pliers and Mini Wire Cutters
  • Jewelry Chain and Necklace Parts
  • Jacquard Pearl-Ex Pigment
  • Plastic Water Cup
  • Jewelry Adhesive
  • Disposable Cup

Steps

  1. Cover the work surface with a protective covering.
  2. Mix 2 parts resin and 1 part hardener in disposable cup. Stir vigorously.
  3. Pour glitter into resin molds, then pour in your resin mixture. Pour to the top, but make sure you do not over pour. Then stir the glitter around
  4. To tint the resin, pour a small amount of resin into the short plastic water cup and add a few shakes of Pearl-Ex pigment. Pour into the mold.
  5. Put the mold in a warm, dry place. The resin will take approximately 24–36 hours to harden. Once the resin is glass-hard, put the mold in the freezer for a few minutes. Then pop out.
  6. The edges of the gems will be sharp. Trim the edges with scissors or smooth them by wetting fine grit sandpaper and rubbing the edges in a gentle circular motion.
  7. To make into jewelry, glue metal bead caps to the top of each piece with E6000 glue. Then, use long-nose pliers to attach jump rings for use with chains or earring hooks.

TRINKET SPOON JEWELRY RESIN NECKLACE

"

What you will need.

  • EnviroTex Jewelry Resin or:
    • Resin
    • Measuring Cups
  • Trinkets
    • Sprockets
    • Cogs
    • Filigrees
    • Jewelry Pieces
    • Sea Shells
    • Sea Stars
    • Studs
    • Anything Small
    • Spoons
    • Chain/Necklace findings
    • E6000 Glue
    • Mixing cups, sticks and disposable gloves
    • Torch/Hammer/Axe

    Steps

    1. Use a torch and heat the handle of the spoon until red hot. Hold onto the spoon tightly with pliers. Hold onto the spoon tightly with pliers.
    2. Immediately after removing from the heat, place the spoon on the edge of an axe and hammer down on it once to cut the metal in half.
    3. Heat the remaining handle up again in the torch until red hot. Then hammer the stem around a metal rod to make it round.
    4. Gather all the trinkets and glue them to the insides of the spoons. Let dry.
    5. Mix the resin according to the package directions. I only needed 6 drams (not quite 1 ounce) of mixed resin for these 3 spoons. Wear gloves and work in a well ventilated area.
    6. Use a stick to gently fill the spoon with resin. The resin should fill the spoon and count on surface tension for the best look.
    7. Then be patient and let the resin dry completely–overnight should do it.
    8. Finally, hook the spoons on necklace chains or a velvet choker.
